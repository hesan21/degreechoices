Progressivism and Academia:
The Most Progressive Universities in the US

What is the most progressive university in the US? It’s a question that’s often asked but rarely answered in any satisfactory way. Although the question of progressivism is open ended and subjective to some extent, we have endeavored to define the most progressive institutions based on a variety of metrics.

Methodology

We surveyed the 30 top-rated universities in the US and assessed their levels of progressivism by focusing on the following:

Diversity

Freedom of Speech

Inclusivity

Health and Wellbeing

Sustainability

These metrics were supplemented by scores relating to health and wellbeing. Data was collected from primary and secondary sources, including university policy pages and external organizations such as STARS, IV Stats, and College Factual. Data was collected in its raw form before being normalized to allow for comparisons. Finally, we categorized the normalized data to allow for easier reading and comparisons. Each university was then given an overall progressiveness rating based on its scores across all categories.

Why Progressivism
Important in Education?

Academic environments are not only designed to be bastions of education but safe spaces where people of all types can flourish. According to industry body Pearson, the original raison d’être for universities was to be guardians of “reason, inquiry, and philosophical openness.” This ethos has since been combined with a more modern purpose, namely the facilitation of social mobility. Universities are not only places to exchange and examine ideas but allow people from all backgrounds to change their lives. A degree is an investment that students believe will pay dividends in the future. This is why progressivism is integral to everything a university does. Only by focusing on issues such as diversity, inclusivity, sustainability, and freedom of speech can an institution hope to uphold its core values. Our study has ranked universities across the US based on a variety of metrics. No single rating can be taken as a definitive conclusion on how progressive an institution is. Moreover, each category considers a variety of points. Therefore, it may be the case that an institution thrives in one area but not another. However, what we hope to achieve with this study is an overview of progressivism in academia. Moreover, our findings can be used to determine the universities that best embrace the ideals of social justice, change, and self-improvement, thus fulfilling the true raison d’être of higher education.

Findings

Although it didn’t score highly with respect to freedom of speech and diversity, Stanford is strong in virtually all other areas. Despite having a fairly low acceptance rate of 4%, it still managed to score 89.20% for inclusivity due to robust system of grants and scholarships. It’s also the top US university for sustainability and it offers a safe learning environment. Indeed, our tests show that it has a 90.25% score for health and wellbeing thanks to personalized coaching and counselling services. Students can book sessions with support specialists from a variety of backgrounds, ethnicities, and sexual orientations. The aim is to provide a personal, social, and clinical points of contact for all students, regardless of their background. Finally, Stanford has one of the lowest crime rates in the country. In 2019 there were just 268 reported crimes. At the lowest rated university, NYU, there were 1,761 reported crimes during the same period.

Findings

The California Institute of Technology (Caltech) narrowly edged out New York University as the most diverse college in the US. Its score of 58.59% was thanks, in part, to its current diversity policy and, in turn, the demographics of its student body. As per its Center for Inclusion and Diversity, Caltech abides by state and federal laws, including Title IX, at all levels. This includes students, faculty, researchers, and staff. This commitment to diversity has led to a student population that’s 54.7% men and 45.3% women. Of the 2,237 undergraduate and graduate students, 27% are white. Overall, seven racial-ethnic groups are represented at Caltech, with Asian and black students accounting for a total of 53.8% of the student population. In contrast, the University of Notre Dame, which had the lowest diversity score (10.81%), has a white student population of 63.5%.
